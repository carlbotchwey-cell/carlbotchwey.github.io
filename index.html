Welcome.

I am Carl Botchwey, a PhD Candidate in Accounting at Université Clermont Auvergne, France. My research explores the reporting and dissemination of corporate information in capital markets — with particular attention to earnings management, analyst forecast behavior, institutional environments, and sustainability reporting.

Before beginning my PhD, I earned a Master of Commerce and a Bachelor of Commerce in Accounting from the University of Cape Coast, Ghana — graduating with distinction. I am also an Associate Member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants, Ghana.

Working Papers

Market Reaction to Earnings Announcements News of Cross-Listings: A Comparative Analysis of European Firms and Their Sponsored ADRs

Carl Botchwey and François Aubert Under review Review of Quantitative Finance and Accounting

This study investigates the asymmetric market responses to earnings announcements by comparing European-listed firms with their sponsored American Depositary Receipts (ADRs). Leveraging a novel paired-sample design, we isolate the effect of institutional and regulatory environments on investor behavior. ADRs elicit significantly stronger positive reactions to earnings surprises and more severe penalizations for earnings management relative to their European counterparts, supporting the Bonding and Investor Recognition Hypotheses.

Pre-disclosure Information Asymmetry and Cross-Listings

Carl Botchwey and François Aubert Working paper

Using a within-firm paired design, this study compares analyst forecasts for ADRs and their European counterparts. Forecasts associated with ADRs exhibit modestly higher accuracy and substantially lower disagreement, suggesting stronger convergence of analyst expectations within the U.S. cross-listed environment.
