Working Papers

1. Market Reaction to Earnings Announcements News of Cross-Listings

(Carl Botchwey and François Aubert)
Under review at Review of Quantitative Finance and Accounting.

Abstract: This study investigates the asymmetric market responses to earnings announcements by comparing European-listed firms with their sponsored American Depositary Receipts (ADRs). Leveraging a novel paired-sample design, we control for firm fundamentals to isolate institutional and regulatory effects. ADRs elicit significantly stronger positive reactions to earnings surprises and more severe penalizations for earnings management relative to their European counterparts. Results highlight the role of U.S. institutional frameworks in amplifying investor sensitivity to earnings quality.

2. Pre-disclosure Information Asymmetry and Cross-Listings

(Carl Botchwey and François Aubert)

Abstract: This study examines whether listing venue influences financial analysts’ forecasting behavior by comparing U.S.-listed ADRs with the European-listed shares of the same firm. Using a paired within-firm design, we isolate institutional effects from firm fundamentals. Forecasts associated with ADRs exhibit modestly higher accuracy and substantially lower disagreement. The study highlights the importance of distinguishing informational convergence from behavioural convergence when evaluating cross-listing effects.
